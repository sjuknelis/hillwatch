Privacy Policy

This privacy policy for Hill Watch, describes how and why Hill Watch might collect, store, use, and/or share (“process“) your information when you download and use the Hill Watch browser extension.

Reading this privacy policy will help you understand your privacy rights and choices. If you do not agree with this policy, please do not use Hill Watch. If you still have any questions or concerns, please contact Hill Watch.

Data collection

When you click the Hill Watch icon in your browser, Hill Watch automatically collects the title of the web page that you are currently viewing. This is so that Hill Watch can determine what legislation is relevant to the page that you are viewing. For processing, this data is sent to Hill Watch’s servers and to OpenAI. When this data is sent to OpenAI, it is subject to OpenAI’s privacy policy.

Hill Watch may request access or permission to location-based information from your device. Hill Watch uses GPS and other technologies to collect geolocation data that tells us your current location This is used to determine your congressional district and the identity of your representative in Congress. Location-based information is never sent to Hill Watch’s servers or anywhere outside of your device. You can opt out of allowing Hill Watch to collect this information either by refusing access to the information or by disabling your Location setting on your device. However, if you choose to opt out, Hill Watch may not function.

Hill Watch does not process sensitive information.

Hill Watch automatically collects certain information when you use or navigate Hill Watch. This information does not reveal your specific identity (like your name or contact information) but may include device and usage information, such as your IP address, browser and device characteristics, operating system, language preferences, referring URLs, device name, country, location, information about how and when you use Hill Watch, and other technical information. This information is primarily needed to maintain the security and operation of Hill Watch, and for our internal analytics and reporting purposes.

Do-Not-Track setting

Most web browsers and some mobile operating systems and mobile applications include a Do-Not-Track (“DNT”) feature or setting you can activate to signal your privacy preference not to have data about your online browsing activities monitored and collected. At this stage no uniform technology standard for recognizing and implementing DNT signals has been finalized. As such, Hill Watch does not currently respond to DNT browser signals or any other mechanism that automatically communicates your choice not to be tracked online. If a standard for online tracking is adopted that Hill Watch must follow in the future, Hill Watch will inform you about that practice in a revised version of this privacy notice.
